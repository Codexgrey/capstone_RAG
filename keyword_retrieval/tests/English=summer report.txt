the international research center for AI and IoT . The main project I contributed to was the

1.1 International Research Center for AI and IoT profile  
The international research center for AI and IoT was established at Near East University in 
Cyprus as a place for innovation, advanced research and the development of technologies in 
Artificial Intelligence (AI) and the Internet of Things (IoT). The center brings together 
researchers, engineers and students to work as a team on projects that connect academic 
research with real-world use. 
It works in different areas including automation, smart systems, language technology and 
data analysis. The center also trains students by giving them real hands-on experience in 
software engineering, embedded systems and AI preparing them for future technical jobs 
One of the center’s main goals is to build AI applications that make work easier and more 
efficient. During my training period I work in the team of building AI slide generator is a 
web tools that is going to work with a new educational robot called Prof. DUX is a module 
of generating response and interactive slide pages from users inputs such as text, images and 
other media and these slides are then displayed on the robot’s screen during its teaching 
activities.

platform created to make the process of designing presentation slides faster and easier. The 
system combined artificial intelligence with customization tools, allowing users to quickly 
generate professional and ready-to-use presentation slides.

-Build a user-friendly frontend interface where users could generate, save, customize slides

full-screen presentation mode to make the platform more versatile.

3.3 Limitations and Experience Gained 
Problems Faced: During my internship, I encountered several limitations and technical 
challenges while developing and integrating complex features into the AI Slide Generator 
application. These difficulties allowed me to improve my technical problem-solving skills 
and strengthen my teamwork abilities: 
 
-Theme System Complexity: The implementation of multiple unique cover pages and 
preview images for each presentation theme presented significant design and performance 
challenges. Each theme was designed to keep fonts, colors and layout looking good and 
working well on all devices. 
Solution: The issue was resolved by modularizing the theme configuration files and 
optimizing CSS layers in Tailwind. This allowed independent control of each theme’s visual 
attributes and improved maintainability. 
 
-Docker Performance Issues: Running Docker containers on local machines caused 
performance degradation, particularly when multiple containers were active simultaneously. 
This slowed down testing and debugging processes. 
Solution: To overcome this, lightweight images were used, and unnecessary background 
containers were disabled. Adjusting system resource allocation and leveraging .dockerignore 
also enhanced performance. 
 
-API communication errors: While connecting the frontend and backend, some endpoints 
failed because of incorrect request data or mismatched authentication tokens. 
Solution: Postman was used to test and automate the endpoints. Adding detailed error logs 
and using consistent response formats made debugging easier and reduced integration 
problems. 
 
-OpenAI integration and response Validation: Integrating OpenAI APIs introduced 
challenges in handling unpredictable or incomplete AI responses. Ensuring that generated 
slide content met the project’s quality standards required advanced validation mechanisms. 
Solution: A two-stage prompt system was introduced one for slide structure and another for 
detailed notes combined with server-side validation checks to filter incomplete responses. 
This significantly improved AI output accuracy and reliability.

effective coordination and communication. Differences in coding styles and task priorities 
occasionally slowed down progress. 
Solution: The team adopted agile methodologies with daily stand-up meetings, regular sprint 
reviews and collaborative debugging sessions. Version control using GitLab ensured smooth 
integration and prevented conflicts between team members contributions. 
 
In addition to the technical challenges adapting to a fast-paced development environment 
proved difficult at first. Balancing multiple tasks, managing unexpected bugs and keeping
