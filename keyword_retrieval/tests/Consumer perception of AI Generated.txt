Consumer Perception of AI-Generated Marketing Content Compared to 
Human-Created Content 
1. Introduction 
In the modern digital marketing environment, artificial intelligence has become an important tool 
for creating marketing content. Companies increasingly use AI technologies to produce 
advertisements, social media posts, product descriptions and promotional messages. AI allows 
businesses to generate large volumes of content quickly and at a lower cost compared to traditional 
human content creation. 
Despite these advantages, the growing use of AI in marketing raises important questions about how 
consumers perceive such content. Marketing communication is not only about delivering 
information but also about creating trust, emotional connection, and brand identity. Human creators 
often bring creativity, cultural understanding, and emotional depth to marketing messages. In 
contrast, AI-generated content may sometimes appear efficient but less authentic. 
This situation creates what researchers call an “authenticity paradox.” While AI can produce high-
quality and grammatically perfect content, consumers may still prefer human-created messages 
because they perceive them as more genuine and emotionally engaging. At the same time, many 
consumers interact daily with AI-generated content without even realizing it. 
Understanding how consumers react to these two types of marketing content is crucial for companies 
that want to design effective marketing strategies. If consumers trust and engage with AI-generated 
content, businesses may increasingly rely on AI tools. However, if consumers perceive AI content 
as less authentic or manipulative, companies may need to balance AI efficiency with human 
creativity. 
For these reasons, studying consumer perception of AI-generated marketing content compared to 
human-created content has become a significant research topic in the field of digital media and 
marketing. 
 
2. Research problem and objectives 
A) Research problem 
The rapid adoption of artificial intelligence in marketing has created uncertainty regarding how 
consumers perceive AI-generated messages. Although AI allows companies to produce marketing 
materials quickly and efficiently, it remains unclear whether consumers trust these messages as 
much as content created by humans. 
Some studies suggest that when consumers know that content has been generated by AI, they may 
perceive it as less authentic or trustworthy. In certain contexts, such as healthcare or financial 
services, this perception may significantly influence purchasing decisions. Additionally, as AI-
generated advertisements become more common, consumers may experience digital fatigue, leading 
them to prefer marketing messages that appear more human and emotionally engaging. 


Another challenge is that consumers may interpret AI-generated content as a cost-cutting strategy 
rather than an effort to provide value. This perception could potentially affect brand credibility and 
long-term customer relationships. 
Therefore, the main research problem of this study is to determine whether AI-generated marketing 
content influences consumer perception, trust, and purchasing behaviour differently than human-
created marketing content. 
B) Research Objectives 
The primary objective of this research is to analyse and compare consumer perceptions of AI-
generated and human-created marketing content. 
The specific objectives include: 
1. To examine how consumers perceive AI-generated marketing content in terms of quality, 
authenticity, and credibility. 
2. To compare consumer trust and engagement levels between AI-generated content and 
human-created marketing content. 
3. To analyse whether transparency, such as labelling content as AI-generated, influences 
consumer attitudes and purchase intentions. 
4. To identify which types of marketing content (text, visuals, or videos) are more acceptable 
to consumers when created by AI. 
5. To understand the role of emotional connection and storytelling in shaping consumer 
preferences for human-created marketing messages. 
By addressing these objectives, this research aims to provide valuable insights into how artificial 
intelligence can be effectively integrated into digital marketing strategies. 
 
3. Research Methodology 
To investigate consumer perception of AI-generated and human-created marketing content, this 
study will use a mixed-method research design, combining quantitative and qualitative approaches. 
This approach will allow researchers to obtain both measurable results and deeper insights into 
consumer attitudes. 
A) Research Design 
The quantitative part of the research will involve an experimental survey design. Participants will 
be divided into three groups: 
-Group A: Participants exposed to marketing content labelled as human-created. 
-Group B: Participants exposed to marketing content labelled as AI-generated. 
-Group C: Participants exposed to similar marketing content without any label (control 
group). 


After viewing the content, participants will evaluate it using rating scales measuring factors such 
as: 
➢ Trustworthiness 
➢ Authenticity 
➢ Creativity 
➢ Emotional impact 
➢ Purchase intention 
 
The qualitative phase will include semi-structured interviews and focus group discussions. These 
sessions will explore participants’ opinions about the role of AI in marketing, their feelings toward 
AI-generated content, and their expectations for authentic brand communication. 
B) Theoretical Framework 
The study will also be supported by two important theoretical models commonly used in technology 
and marketing research: 
The Technology Acceptance Model (TAM) helps explain how people accept and use new 
technologies by analysing perceived usefulness and ease of use. 
The Persuasion Knowledge Model (PKM) explains how consumers interpret persuasive messages 
and how they react when they recognize marketing attempts. 
These frameworks will help analyse how consumers evaluate AI-generated marketing messages and 
how awareness of AI influences their responses. 
 
4. Data Collection 
A) Secondary Data 
Secondary data will be collected through a review of existing academic and industry research. 
Sources will include: 
-Academic journals related to digital marketing and consumer behaviour 
-Industry reports on AI adoption in marketing 
-Research articles discussing algorithm aversion and consumer trust in automated systems 
-Case studies of companies using AI in marketing campaigns 
In addition, online discussions and social media trends related to AI marketing will be analysed to 
understand current public attitudes. 
B) Primary Data 
Primary data will be collected through online surveys and experimental questionnaires distributed 
through digital platforms such as university networks and social media. The study aims to collect 
responses from approximately 500 participants, mainly from Generation Z and Millennial 
consumers, who are highly exposed to digital technologies. 


Participants will be asked to evaluate marketing content and answer questions using rating scales 
measuring their perceptions and emotional reactions. 
If resources permit, a smaller group of participants may participate in in-depth interviews or focus 
groups to provide deeper insights into their attitudes toward AI-generated marketing. 
 
5. Data Analysis 
The quantitative data collected from surveys will be analysed using statistical methods such as T-
tests and analysis of variance (ANOVA) to determine whether significant differences exist between 
consumer responses to AI-generated and human-created content. 
Qualitative data from interviews and discussions will be analysed using thematic analysis, which 
identifies recurring themes and patterns in participants’ opinions about authenticity, trust, creativity, 
and emotional connection in marketing content. 
 
Conclusion 
As artificial intelligence continues to transform digital marketing, understanding consumer 
perception becomes increasingly important. While AI offers efficiency and scalability, human 
creativity still plays a crucial role in building emotional connections with consumers. 
This research aims to provide insights into how consumers evaluate AI-generated marketing content 
compared to human-created content. The findings may help marketers determine how to effectively 
combine artificial intelligence and human creativity to design marketing strategies that are both 
efficient and authentic.
